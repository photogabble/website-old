Reading List.

Below is a list of books on my reading list. Those with accompanying review links are listed in the order in which they were read.

  • The Danish Way of Parenting

    Alexander, J. and Sandahl, I. (2016). The Danish way of parenting. London: Piatkus.

  • 15 million degrees: a journey to the centre of the Sun

    Lucie Green. (2017). 15 million degrees: a journey to the centre of the Sun. London: Penguin Books.

  • Storm in a teacup

    Helen Czerski. (2016). Storm in a teacup : the physics of everyday life. London: Black Swan 2017.

  • The infinite monkey cage: how to build a universe

    Professor Brian Cox, Robin Ince and Alexandra Feachem. (2018). The infinite monkey cage: how to build a universe. London: William Collins 2018.

  • The Beekeepers Club

    Bill Turnbull. (2011). The bad beekeepers club: how I stumbled into the curious world of bees - and became (perhaps) a better person. London: Sphere.

  • How to teach relativity to your dog

    Chad Orzel. (2012). How to teach relativity to your dog. New York: Basic Books.

  • Physics of the impossible

    Michio Kaku. (2009). Physics of the impossible: A scientific exploration of the world of phasers, force fields, teleportation and time travel. London: Penguin Books.

  • The Martian Chronicles

    Ray Bradbury. (2008). The Martian Chronicles. London: Harper Collins Publishers.

  • The particle at the end of the universe

    Sean Carroll. (2013). The particle at the end of the universe: The hunt for the Higgs and the discovery of a new world. London: Oneworld Publications.

  • Nothing: From absolute zero to cosmic oblivion

    New Scientist. (2013). Nothing: From absolute zero to cosmic oblivion - amazing insights into nothingness. London: Profile Books Ltd.
